On Tuesday, a 45-year-old man was critically injured when he was pushed in front of a train at the 18th Street subway station in Manhattan's Chelsea neighborhood. The incident, captured on surveillance video, shows the victim standing on the platform looking at his phone when a man, identified as 23-year-old Kamel Hawkins, pushed him onto the tracks just as a train was arriving. The victim sustained a skull fracture and remains in critical condition at Bellevue Hospital. Hawkins has been arrested and charged with attempted murder and assault. Authorities believe the attack was unprovoked and that the victim did not know Hawkins. The incident has left subway riders shaken, with many expressing concerns about safety on the platforms
